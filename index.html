What's happening
Drag the throttle (THR) and prop lever (RPM) to see the system respond. Push both forward for climb power, pull both back for efficient cruise.
Incorrect lever configuration
How to use this
Drag the THR lever to change manifold pressure — this controls how much air-fuel charge the engine receives per stroke.
Drag the RPM lever to set the governor's target RPM. The constant-speed unit then automatically adjusts blade pitch to hold exactly that RPM.
Watch the propeller: fine pitch blades appear thin (edge-on to airflow). Coarse pitch blades open up their faces — more air mass per revolution at lower RPM.
Try setting high throttle with low RPM, or low throttle with high RPM — the red warning box explains the ATPL-level hazard of each wrong combination.
